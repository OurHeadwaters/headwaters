# Physical Kit — Hard Assets, Energy, and Contingency Planning

## What This Kit Does

The Physical Kit covers hard assets, energy independence, and real preparedness. From off-grid power systems to gear selection, contingency planning, and the Standby protocols that matter in Northern Ontario winters — this is the kit for building physical resilience that works when the grid does not.

Physical resilience is not about fear. It is about confidence. People who have thought through contingencies and built real capabilities are calmer, more decisive, and more effective when things go wrong.

## Core Pillars

### 1. Grid Independence
Most households are one extended power outage away from a crisis. Grid independence starts with understanding your actual power needs, identifying the critical loads you cannot live without, and building a backup system that covers those loads for at least 72 hours — and ideally much longer.

### 2. Energy Production
Solar, wind, and micro-hydro are the three practical options for residential energy production. Steven Harris has taught on TSP dozens of times on exactly how to size, spec, and install these systems. This kit gives you his framework.

### 3. Energy Storage
Batteries are the heart of any off-grid or backup power system. Battery chemistry, capacity, depth of discharge, and the real-world performance differences between options. What to buy at each price point.

### 4. Hard Assets
Gold and silver have preserved purchasing power for thousands of years. This kit covers the basics: what forms to buy, where to store them, how much to hold, and how to think about the ratio between precious metals, crypto, and cash.

### 5. Contingency Planning — Standby Protocols
The Standby protocols are a set of decision frameworks developed for Northern Ontario conditions: extended power outages, severe weather, supply chain disruption, and communication failure. They are not survival fiction — they are operational plans for real-world scenarios.

### 6. Gear Selection
The gear shelf matters here more than in any other kit. Energy equipment is expensive and the wrong choice is costly. This kit connects you to Jack's reviewed equipment with specific model recommendations for your situation.

## Starting Point

Start the When Things Get Hard track. It is short and covers the high-probability scenarios most families should prepare for.

Work through the Grid → Off-Grid transformation for energy independence concepts, then use the gear shelf to identify the right hardware for your situation and budget.

## What Done Looks Like

- 72-hour power backup in place for critical loads
- Understanding of your full household energy needs documented
- At least one energy production option assessed for your property
- Hard asset position started (even a small amount of silver)
- Standby protocols written and shared with your household
- Gear shelf reviewed and highest-priority equipment acquired
