# Parr's Jars — Food Preservation Course

## What This Kit Does

Parr's Jars is Bobbie Parr's ten-session food preservation course from Dryden, Ontario. It covers canning, fermenting, and stocking a real northern pantry — taught from a kitchen that has been doing this since 2020.

This is practitioner-grade instruction. Bobbie is not a lifestyle blogger. She is a northern homesteader who puts up hundreds of jars every season because her family depends on what is in those jars.

## The Ten Sessions

### Session 1: Philosophy of the Pantry
Why you stock a pantry, how to think about it as a system, and the difference between a crisis pantry and a living pantry. This session sets the foundation for everything that follows.

### Session 2: Equipment Fundamentals
What you actually need versus what the canning industry wants you to buy. Jar selection, lid quality, canner types, and the tools that earn their place in a small kitchen.

### Session 3: Water Bath Canning
The gateway skill. High-acid foods — tomatoes, pickles, jams, fruits — processed safely in a water bath canner. Safety protocols, processing times, and testing for a proper seal.

### Session 4: Pressure Canning
Low-acid foods require pressure canning: meats, beans, vegetables, soups, and stocks. This session covers the Presto canner Bobbie uses, dial versus weighted gauge canners, and the specific protocols for northern Canadian kitchens.

### Session 5: Fermentation Fundamentals
Lacto-fermentation is the oldest food preservation method and one of the most nutritionally valuable. Sauerkraut, kimchi, fermented vegetables, and brine ratios for a northern climate.

### Session 6: Dehydrating and Freeze-Drying
Removing moisture to extend shelf life. Dehydrating fruits, vegetables, and meats at home. How freeze-drying compares and when it is worth the investment.

### Session 7: Building Your Rotation System
A pantry that does not rotate is a pantry that wastes food. This session covers the first-in-first-out system Bobbie uses, labeling conventions, and how to meal plan around your pantry stock.

### Session 8: Northern Produce and Wild Foods
Foraging for preservation: berries, mushrooms, and wild plants that are abundant in the boreal region. How to safely identify, harvest, and preserve foods you did not grow or buy.

### Session 9: Pantry for a Household
Scaling your pantry for your actual household — size, dietary needs, budget, and storage constraints. How to build a one-year pantry over 12 months without breaking the bank.

### Session 10: Teaching Your Household
A pantry is a household system. This session covers how to bring your partner, kids, and extended family into the practice so it becomes a shared skill, not a solo project.

## Starting Point

Watch Session 1 before you buy anything. The philosophy of stocking a real pantry matters more than which lids you use. Work through the sessions in order — each one builds on the last.

## What Done Looks Like

- 90-day food supply preserved at home
- At least three different preservation methods in active use
- Rotation system in place — food moves through the pantry, not into it
- At least one other household member participating in the practice
- Session 10 complete — the skill is shared, not siloed
