# Digital Kit — Financial and Digital Sovereignty

## What This Kit Does

The Digital Kit is your complete guide to financial and digital sovereignty. It covers privacy, self-custody, on-chain tools, secure communication, and the practical steps to reduce your dependence on big tech and traditional banks.

This is not a technical kit for programmers. It is a practical kit for people who have decided they want to own their digital life — including their money — and are willing to do the work to make that happen.

## Core Pillars

### 1. Self-Custody Fundamentals
If you do not hold your keys, you do not hold your coins. Self-custody starts with a hardware wallet, a secure seed phrase backup, and a clear understanding of what you are responsible for. This kit walks you through the setup process step by step.

### 2. Privacy Infrastructure
Your digital privacy is not binary — every improvement reduces your exposure. Start with the basics: a privacy-respecting browser and search engine, a VPN from a trusted provider, encrypted messaging for your close contacts, and a password manager. Build from there.

### 3. Secure Communication
Signal for messaging. ProtonMail or Tutanota for email. A private voice option for sensitive calls. Most people resist these changes because they require coordination with others — this kit helps you make the case and make the transition.

### 4. Financial Sovereignty Stack
Bitcoin for savings and long-term wealth preservation. XRP and RLUSD for liquidity and payments. Hard assets (gold, silver) as the physical anchor. This kit maps the complete financial sovereignty stack and tells you what to prioritize in what order.

### 5. Reducing Big Tech Dependence
De-Googling, de-Amazoning, and finding alternatives to the surveillance capitalism platforms. This is a long project — the kit breaks it into phases so you can make progress without disrupting your life all at once.

## Starting Point

Start with the TradFi → Hard Assets transformation episodes. Understand the why before touching a hardware wallet. The conceptual foundation determines whether you will actually execute the rest.

Set up a hardware wallet with a small amount before you hold anything significant. Mistakes with crypto are permanent — learn the system with a small test amount first.

## What Done Looks Like

- Hardware wallet set up with test transaction completed
- Seed phrase backed up in a secure physical location
- Privacy-respecting browser, search, and email in use
- Signal installed and at least 5 close contacts switched to it
- Financial sovereignty stack documented — what you hold, where, and why
- Big tech dependence reduced in at least three categories
