# Council Kit — Community-Scale Resilience

## What This Kit Does

The Council Kit is for communities ready to build real resilience at scale. It provides a 4-phase engagement system for councils, co-ops, and leadership groups that want to move beyond talk into coordinated, self-reliant action.

This is not a program for individuals. It is a framework for groups of people who have decided to build something together — a local food network, a community energy co-op, a regional preparedness council, or a sovereign community hub.

## The 4-Phase Engagement System

### Phase 1: Assessment
Before you can build anything, you need to know where you actually stand. The assessment phase maps your community's current capabilities, dependencies, vulnerabilities, and assets. Most communities are surprised by how much they already have.

### Phase 2: Foundation
Build the institutional infrastructure that makes collective action possible. This includes governance structures, communication protocols, shared resources, and the trust frameworks that allow people to coordinate without central authority.

### Phase 3: Production
Move from planning to doing. The production phase focuses on the specific resilience capacities your community has identified as highest priority — food production, energy independence, health infrastructure, financial resilience, or community security.

### Phase 4: Integration
Resilience at scale is not a project. It is a way of operating. The integration phase embeds the practices, rhythms, and systems from phases 1–3 into your community's ongoing life, so they persist and evolve without constant outside input.

## Who This Is For

- Municipal and regional councils exploring community resilience frameworks
- Agricultural co-operatives building local food sovereignty
- Indigenous community organizations reclaiming traditional self-reliance practices
- Faith communities building resilience for their members and neighbors
- Neighborhood associations and community development organizations

## Access

The Council Kit is consultative — it requires an initial conversation to scope the engagement correctly for your community's size, context, and readiness. Contact us through the inquiry form to begin.

## What Done Looks Like

- Community resilience assessment completed and shared with all stakeholders
- Governance structure in place with clear decision-making protocols
- At least one priority resilience capacity built to a functional level
- Ongoing community rhythm established for maintaining and expanding resilience
- Other communities able to learn from and adapt your model
