## What a Kit Is
A trade instrument — not a program, subscription, or service. Like a carpenter's kit: learnable, named, passable across a threshold.

Every kit is folded in two sides:
- **Heart side** — a named, bounded container for the grief, weight, or fear the person is carrying. Named. Witnessed. Bounded (it has a bottom). Not permanent residence — passage.
- **Hands side** — practical, learnable moves. A loop, not a checklist. Something a practitioner can hold and transfer. Delivered as a companion digital tool.

Kits are Gate-compliant: they don't demand the person cross vocabulary they don't have. Protected meaning in plain form.

Kits are **not crisis tools**. Used in the aftermath, or before the season arrives. They build the floor before it's needed.

## Business Model
- **Apps are free. Complete. Full value.** A family can spend a decade inside the ecosystem without paying and get everything.
- **Kits are the easy button** — curated depth for people who want to go further without building from scratch.
- Kits live and are sold at **headwaters.ca**. Inside the free apps, surfaced at the right moment — never pushed, never paywalled. A single link out, at a crossing.

## The Arch and the Eave
> The apps are the eave. The kits are the arch. They work together. They are not the same thing.

## The Moments Philosophy
Every kit has a **moment** — a life moment, a crossing. Not a marketing moment.

"Moments of Joy" (dementia care philosophy: meet someone exactly where they are in time and space) is the north star for how kits appear inside apps.

The question is never "where can we mention the kit?" The question is: **what is the crossing, and when does the person know they are about to make it?** Surface the kit there, and only there.

**The Goodbye Kit is the canonical exemplar:** surfaces in The Eave app *before* a circle closes — before the dissolve decision, at the moment of intention, when the person still has time to decide whether to walk through it alone or ask a friend to hold their hand.

## Life Stages Arc (kit organization)
Kits are organized by life crossings, not topics:

**prenatal → birth → naming → first blood → apprenticeship → union → loss → elderhood → dying**

Some kits span multiple crossings. Some are for the caregivers standing beside the crosser, not the crosser themselves.

## The Ladder Analogy
The practitioner (via source work and self-location) is dropped at the top of the ladder. From there they shimmy freely, handing individual ladders to every person they meet — right where that person stands. The kits are the ladders. Each designed for one rung, one life stage, one kind of weight. The practitioner doesn't pull the person up; they hand them a ladder that fits, with a carabiner already clipped.

The machete: the practitioner clears the path (deadheads the snakes) before the family even sees them.

## Constitutional Rules for Agents
1. **Never add a kit surface that isn't attached to a named life moment or crossing.**
2. **Never gate app features behind kit purchase.** The app is always complete and free.
3. **The kit link is always a single, calm pointer outward.** No banners. No upsells. No urgency language.
4. **Tone is practitioner** — warm, quiet competence, grounded. Never promotional.

**Why:** These rules are load-bearing. Violating them breaks the trust model the entire product is built on.

**How to apply:** Before any kit surfacing feature, name the specific life crossing it attaches to. If you can't name it, don't surface it.
